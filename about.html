About

Hey, I am Camie, a linguist and cognitive scientist interested in everything related to language, cognition and evolution, from the neural correlates of language to linguistic complexity, from comparative cognition to human evolution, and from embodied cognition to multimodal language. I hold a BA in Modern Languages and Literature from the University of Pisa, and an MSc in Linguistics from the University of Edinburgh. In my research, I privilege domains and methodologies which allow to connect in an original and productive way disciplines and approaches apparently unrelated, integrating concepts from Philosophy, Anthropology, Psychology and Artificial Intelligence. I use behavioural experiments as well as computational models to simulate linguistic dynamics. On a more personal note, I consider myself to be a curious, energetic and motivated individual with a passion for learning and discovering new things.

When I am not working you can find me doing a mix of other things: running, while listening to epic soundtracks and taking pictures of the scenery; watching Netflix, while sipping coffee or tea (depending on the time of day); or attempting to teach myself Tai Chi and Japanese Calligraphy. I also enjoy art, music and history. I am an avid reader of British literature.
